Filling the Shelves

By Julia Moss

A bell rings; cans and boxes are packed and labeled pink, yellow or green according to family size. Small groups move in and out as volunteers make appointments and wheel groceries to clients’ cars. Programs such as C.A.R.E United Methodist Outreach in McArthur, Ohio are doing what they can to include whole foods, but ultimately they rely on food banks to supply overstock or donated groceries at 19 cents a pound. If the food banks don’t have a supply of whole food to fit demand, then pantries are left giving out pastries when there is no more fresh bread on the shelves. Vinton County residents’ access to whole foods was limited when their only grocery store closed in September 2014. For many residents, learning how to cope without easy access to fresh meats and produce hasn’t been easy. When an individual’s transportation fails or becomes inaccessible, they are left relying on friends and family to travel 10 or more miles to the nearest grocery store.
