Farming In The Foothills

By Bri Griesinger

Birds sing early in the morning as hooves clamor back to the barn, the herd manager calls to move them along. A herd of around 50 goats are milked twice a day to keep production flowing at Integration Acres. Producing four types of goat cheese, when Integration Acres was started in 1996 their mission was not only to produce food in Appalachia but also to encourage their community to look back to their roots. Almost 20 years later they are bringing in enough income from their local Appalachian farm to sustain their family and give back to the community of local food producers.
